# Advanced Sections

## Factor and Alpha Modeling

- Chapters 4 and 5 are the technical heart of the stock-selection material.
- The advanced pieces are multi-factor estimation, OLS inference, risk-adjusted alpha, and the cases where the standard regression assumptions break down.
- The nonparametric diagnostics matter because finance data often violates the clean assumptions that make linear models look well-behaved.

## Time-Series and Statistical Arbitrage

- Chapter 6 is the first section that clearly crosses into advanced econometrics.
- Unit roots, spurious regression, and cointegration are foundational if you want to do time-series modeling without fooling yourself.
- The pair-trading material is important because it moves from diagnostics into a tradable structure.

## Portfolio Construction

- Chapter 7 is advanced because it connects forecasts to weights, then forces you to deal with covariance estimation, backtesting, and attribution.
- This is where many otherwise good alpha ideas fail in practice.

## Tail Modeling

- Chapter 8 is one of the strongest advanced chapters in the book.
- CVaR and quantile regression are useful because they focus on the tails instead of average behavior.
- This is especially relevant when you care more about downside shape than about mean accuracy.

## Quantamental Workflow

- Chapter 9 is less mathematical than Chapters 6-8 but still advanced operationally.
- The hard part is not the definition of quantamental investing; it is combining discretionary research with systematic ranking, process, and culture in a disciplined way.

## Best First Pass

- Start with Chapters 4, 6, 7, and 8.
- Add Chapter 5 if you want stronger regression diagnostics.
- Use Chapter 9 once the research process itself becomes the bottleneck.
