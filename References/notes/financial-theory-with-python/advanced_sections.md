# Advanced Sections

## Chapter 4 - Utility, Equilibrium, and Incomplete Markets

- This is advanced because it treats portfolio choice from expected utility first principles instead of using only mean-variance shortcuts.
- The most technical parts are representative-agent equilibrium, martingale measures in incomplete markets, and market completion by contingent claims.

## Chapter 5 - Static Asset Pricing Theory

- This chapter is heavily theory-driven and is likely the most important one if you want the formal no-arbitrage foundation.
- The advanced sections are the Fundamental Theorems of Asset Pricing, replication, arbitrage pricing, completeness, and Arrow-Debreu pricing.
- The BSM and Merton model material matters because it ties discrete theory back to recognizable option-pricing structures.

## Chapter 6 - Dynamic Economy

- This chapter is the most implementation-relevant advanced chapter in the set.
- Binomial trees, Monte Carlo simulation, American option pricing, and Least-Squares Monte Carlo are the main sections worth revisiting.
- If you only study one chapter deeply for practical derivatives work, this is probably the one.

## Suggested Order

- Start with Chapter 5 if you want the cleanest pricing foundation.
- Move to Chapter 6 for actual dynamic pricing methods.
- Use Chapter 4 when you want the optimization and equilibrium layer behind the pricing arguments.
