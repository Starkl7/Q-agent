# Advanced Sections

## Chapter 6 - Alpha Sizing

- Sections 6.3 through 6.6 are the most useful advanced material.
- Risk-based sizing and time-series risk-based portfolio targeting are where the chapter moves beyond general PM advice into actual systematic process design.
- The empirical analysis section is important because it tests sizing rules instead of just asserting them.

## Chapter 9 - Stop-Loss Design

- This chapter is advanced because it treats stop-loss policy as a design problem with explicit trade-offs, not as a slogan.
- The key sections are 9.1 through 9.3: how stop-loss works, why it exists, and what it really costs.
- This is relevant if you care about survival, capital preservation, and how risk rules interact with the strategy edge.

## Chapter 10 - Leverage

- This is the most compact but highest-stakes chapter in the set.
- The technical core is Section 10.1, which frames leverage from the standpoint of risk, GMV, AUM, breadth, and drawdown tolerance.
- It is advanced because leverage mistakes can destroy a strategy even when the underlying ideas are good.

## Best First Pass

- Start with Chapter 6 if position sizing is still heuristic.
- Read Chapter 9 next if you need a more principled stop-loss framework.
- Read Chapter 10 when thinking about fund-level risk posture rather than single-strategy tuning.
